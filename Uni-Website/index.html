Eye Tracking in UX

An introduction into the world of eye tracking

What is Eye Tracking?

Eye tracking is a technology that uses an eye tracking device to track and record eye movement. The data collected from the user research enables designers to understand how people read, what they look at while making decisions, and how they process information. Additionally, eye tracking can be used to study how people interact with technology, such as computers, mobile phones, and virtual reality headsets.

This technique is used in usability studies to track the gaze of a test subject as they use a piece of software or hardware. By tracking the gaze, researchers can identify areas of interest and potential areas of difficulty. This information can be used to improve the design of the software or hardware being tested There are a number of ways to track eye movements including, screen-based, eye tracking glasses and using VR headsets.

Pros and Cons of Eye Tracking

Pros:

  • Provide valuable insights into how people interact with digital devices and applications.
  • Can help identify areas of improvement for digital interfaces.
  • Assess the effectiveness of advertising and other forms of digital marketing.
  • Can help researchers understand human behaviour and cognition.
  • Eye tracking can help researchers understand how people read and comprehend text.

Cons:

  • Can be intrusive and disruptive.
  • Eye tracking can be time-consuming.
  • Can be expensive.
  • Eye tracking can be disturbed by eyeglasses, contact lenses, or other obstructions.
  • People with certain medical conditions, such as nystagmus , may not be able to use eye tracking.
